Martin Luther King Jr.

Civil Rights Activist and Baptist Minister

About Maritin Luther King Jr.

Martin Luther King Jr. was born in January 15, 1929 in Atlanta, Georgia and died in April 4, 1968 after being shot and killed on the balcony of the Lorraine Motel in Memphis Tennessee by, a malcontent drifter and former convict named James Earl Ray. King was a social activist and Baptist minister who played a key role in the American civil rights movement from the mid-1950s until his assassination in 1968. King sought equality and human rights for African Americans, the economically disadvantaged and all victims of injustice through peaceful protest. He was then awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1964 and is remembered each year on Martin Luther King, Jr. Day, a U.S. federal holiday since 1986.

Trivia

  • Martin Luther King Jr. and his father's legal birth name was Michael King, but the elder King changed his and his son’s names to Martin Luther King in honor of the German reformer Martin Luther.
  • King said that his father regularly whipped him until he was fifteen. A neighbor reported hearing the elder King telling his son “he would make something of him even if he had to beat him to death.”
  • At the age of 12, shortly after his maternal grandmother died, King blamed himself and jumped out of a second-story window, but survived.
  • King suffered from depression throughout much of his life.
  • He was the first African American to be granted a national holiday.
  • Martin Luther King Jr. was nearly assassinated a decade earlier than his ultimate death. While on a book tour, on September 20, 1958, King was approached by Izola Ware Curry who asked him if he was Martin Luther King Jr., when he said he was, she said “I’ve been looking for you for five years.” She then pulled out a letter opener and stabbed him in the chest. It took three full hours to remove the blade due to the sharp point end of the blade pressing against his aorta. Doctors had to be extremely careful while removing it because of this. The doctor, Dr. Maynard, told him after, “If you had sneezed during all those hours of waiting, your aorta would have been punctured and you would have drowned in your own blood.”
